ThanhVu (Vu) Nguyen

Bio: ThanhVu (Vu) Nguyen is an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at George Mason University. He completed his Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of New Mexico-Albuquerque and a postdoc at the University of Maryland-College Park. His research lies in the intersection of software engineering and programming languages, with a particular focus on deep neural network verification, dynamic invariant generation, and automatic program repair. He is the recipient of an NSF CAREER Award, an NSF CISE CRII Award, an ACM SIGSOFT/IEEE TCSE 10-year Most Influential Paper Award, an ACM SIGEVO 10-year Impact Paper Award, a Sigma Xi Award for Excellence in Research, an IFIP TC2 Manfred Paul Award for Excellence in Software: Theory and Practice, an ACM SIGEVO "Humies" Gold Medal Award, a CACM Research Highlight, and various Distinguished and Best Paper Awards.

More details

I was born in Saigon, Vietnam, but moved to US at a young age and grew up in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania. I received my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at Penn State-University Park and my M.S. at Penn State-Harrisburg, where I was advised by Thang Bui. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of New Mexico-Albuquerque, where I was mentored by Stephanie Forrest and Deepak Kapur, and completed a two-year postdoc at the University of Maryland-College Park, working with Jeff Foster at PLUM@UMD. Prior to joining George Mason, I was at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. During my grad study, I had the opportunity to intern at the Naval Research Laboratory and Lockheed Martin.
